Screen of shift management function and conventional parameters used to calculate work. Adapting all types of actual shift.

Spending many years of experience in the field with many business models, from standard 3 shifts, 7 crews, working 12 hours off 24 hours , F&B service shifts, sales shifts at shopping centers... We conclude with a common formula so that the software can understand and calculate correctly even when the enterprise applies many different types of shifts at the same time (this is also considered the default because there are bosses in the business who do not have timekeeping , Office workerswork 8 hours of administration, security guards work 12 days off, workers have 3 rotating shifts...)

  • In-Out perious within day: Enterprises arrange human resources to be active in 1 day → Shift concept comes from here. Therefore, the software will manage all working hours, which are statistically grouped, such as "Administrative working hours - Office hours - Office hours – HC"
  • ⏱ Checkin - Checkout time point: It is a fixed point of time when In must be before the specified entry time (5 minutes for example), and Out must be after the required departure time. That's the regulation, but how to ensure all employees to comply with this regulation? Reminders only make the situation more and more out of control, just put a timekeeping device to confirm when the employee swipes on the device. At this time, it is best for workers to be honest with the attendance device, because it is fair and unbiased.
  • ⏱ Time to start recording In: For example, when the office hour is set at 8:00, it is estimated that the earliest employee will be there at 6:00 (the 6:00 mark can be expanded or narrowed depending on the needs of the staff. In fact, enterprises use overtime policy before In...) → employees who swipe their cards before this specified time will not be recorded by the Software as In of administrative working hours.
  • ⏱ Valid Check-In Delay time: It's useful for employee is not counted late by the software because the specified time-in is the Rules - Policy - Internal law, but the employee actually arrived on time but facing with stormy weather or something that delays a few minutes (usually this mark is 5 minutes later)
  • ⏱ Late entry: After the specified time limit is allowed, the Software not only subtracts attendance time but also late to work, but also but also accounting through the rules to calculate the number of times of violation of the timekeeping rules (can submit an explanationby via Submit-Approval flow)
  • ⏱ Checkin expired: Specify the maximum allowed delay time. For example, an Administrative shift that starts at 8:00 will only be counted late until 10:00. If there is a valid reason, the employee may submit a written explanation through the Submit-Approval flow to add valid Entry hours. After this time the Software temporarily serves this working hour and dedicates its performance to other tasks ...
  • ⏱ Begin Checkout: The time when the Software resumes operation after the pause time at the end of the in time and is ready to check the card for the Out time
  • ⏱ Leaving early: A violation of leaving before the specified time is allowed to leave. Employees will be deducted public and also collected as reference when evaluating bonuses and promotions. Use the Submit-Approval flow to present a good reason to not lose effort and avoid Bad grades in future reviews.
  • ⏱ Valid Check-Out Soon time: It is earlier than the Checkout, due to some objective reasons such as the time of the end of the shift, the staff shuttle bus, etc. The enterprise will allow employees to leave earlier than 5 minutes as requested. If this time coincides with the specified Out time, that's fine
  • ⏱ Checkout expired: he maximum time after the Checkout time point (usually after about 02 hours). The software will stop timing Out for this working time frame.
